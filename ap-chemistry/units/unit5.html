Unit 5: Kinetics

Big Picture

Kinetics asks how fast reactions happen and why. Keep separate from thermodynamics: fast/slow is not the same as favorable/unfavorable.

5.1 Reaction Rate

▼
\(\mathrm{Rate=-\frac{1}{a}\frac{d[A]}{dt}=\frac{1}{c}\frac{d[C]}{dt}}\)

5.2 Introduction to Rate Law

▼
\(\mathrm{Rate=k[A]^m[B]^n}\)
  • Orders must be determined experimentally.
Common Mistake

Using coefficients from the balanced equation as orders. Only a proposed elementary mechanism can justify that.

5.3 Concentration Changes Over Time

▼
  • Integrated rate laws relate concentration and time.
  • Half-life behavior depends on reaction order.

5.4 Elementary Reactions

▼
  • An elementary step is a single molecular event.
  • Molecularity applies to elementary steps only.

5.5 Collision Model

▼
  • Effective collisions require sufficient energy and proper orientation.

5.6 Reaction Energy Profile

▼
  • Activation energy barrier controls rate.
  • Catalysts lower activation energy but do not change DeltaH.

5.7 Introduction to Reaction Mechanisms

▼
  • Mechanism steps must sum to the overall reaction.
  • Intermediates are formed and consumed within mechanism steps.

5.8 Reaction Mechanism and Rate Law

▼
  • Rate law predicted by proposed mechanism must match experiment.
  • Slow (rate-determining) step often controls observed rate.
How To Check A Mechanism
  • Add steps and verify overall reaction.
  • Use slow step to derive predicted rate law.
  • Compare with experimental rate law.

5.9 Steady-State Approximation

▼
  • Intermediate concentration is treated as approximately constant during much of reaction.

5.10 Multistep Reaction Energy Profile

▼
  • Each step has its own transition state and activation energy.
  • Highest barrier is typically associated with slow step.

5.11 Catalysis

▼
  • Catalysts provide alternate pathway with lower activation energy.
  • Catalysts are regenerated and not consumed overall.

Unit 5 Extended Notes

▼
More Detail By Topic
  • 5.1-5.3: Rate is a slope idea; rate laws and integrated laws answer different question types.
  • 5.4-5.8: Mechanism validity requires both correct overall equation and correct predicted rate law.
  • 5.9-5.11: Approximation methods and catalysis are modeling tools, not separate laws.

Unit 5 Example Questions and Solutions

▼
Q1: If doubling [A] quadruples rate, what is order in A?
A: Second order in A, since \(2^n=4\Rightarrow n=2\).
Q2: Why can a reaction be thermodynamically favorable but slow?
A: High activation energy can make rate small even when products are energetically favored.
Q3: What does a catalyst change on an energy profile?
A: It lowers \(E_a\), but leaves reactant/product energy difference (\(\Delta H\)) unchanged.
