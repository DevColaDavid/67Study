Unit 9: Thermodynamics and Electrochemistry

Big Picture

Unit 9 combines spontaneity and electricity. First decide if a process is favorable (\(\Delta G\)), then connect that to equilibrium and cell voltage.

9.1 Introduction to Entropy

▼
  • Entropy measures energy dispersal among accessible microstates.
  • Spontaneous processes increase total universe entropy.

9.2 Absolute Entropy and Entropy Change

▼
\(\Delta S^\circ_{\mathrm{rxn}}=\sum nS^\circ_{\mathrm{products}}-\sum nS^\circ_{\mathrm{reactants}}\)

9.3 Gibbs Free Energy and Thermodynamic Favorability

▼
\(\Delta G=\Delta H-T\Delta S\)
  • \(\Delta G<0\) indicates thermodynamic favorability at stated conditions.
Why It Matters

This single equation explains why some reactions are favorable only at high temperature or only at low temperature.

9.4 Thermodynamic and Kinetic Control

▼
  • Thermodynamics predicts favorability; kinetics predicts speed.
  • A favorable reaction can still be slow.

9.5 Free Energy and Equilibrium

▼
\(\Delta G^\circ=-RT\ln K\)
Interpretation
  • If \(K>1\), then \(\ln K>0\), so \(\Delta G^\circ<0\).
  • If \(K<1\), then \(\ln K<0\), so \(\Delta G^\circ>0\).

9.6 Free Energy of Dissolution

▼
  • Dissolution favorability depends on both enthalpy and entropy effects.

9.7 Coupled Reactions

▼
  • Coupling can drive a nonfavorable process if overall DeltaG is negative.

9.8 Galvanic (Voltaic) and Electrolytic Cells

▼
  • Galvanic cells run spontaneous redox and generate electrical work.
  • Electrolytic cells require external electrical input for nonspontaneous redox.

9.9 Cell Potential and Free-Energy

▼
\(\Delta G^\circ=-nFE^\circ_{\mathrm{cell}}\)

9.10 Cell Potential Under Nonstandard Conditions

▼
\(\displaystyle E=E^\circ-\frac{RT}{nF}\ln Q\)
Common Mistake

Using concentration terms with wrong exponents in \(Q\). Build \(Q\) from balanced equation stoichiometry first.

9.11 Electrolysis and Faraday's Law

▼
\(Q=It,\quad \text{moles e}^-=\frac{Q}{F}\)
  • Deposited amount is proportional to passed charge.

Unit 9 Extended Notes

▼
More Detail By Topic
  • 9.1-9.5: Link entropy/enthalpy to \(\Delta G\), then tie \(\Delta G^\circ\) to equilibrium \(K\).
  • 9.6-9.7: Coupling combines favorable and unfavorable processes through shared intermediates.
  • 9.8-9.11: Electrochem translates redox into voltage and charge accounting using Nernst/Faraday relationships.

Unit 9 Example Questions and Solutions

▼
Q1: If \(\Delta G^\circ<0\), what can you say about \(K\)?
A: \(K>1\), from \(\Delta G^\circ=-RT\ln K\).
Q2: In a galvanic cell, where do electrons flow?
A: From anode (oxidation) to cathode (reduction) through external circuit.
Q3: A current of 2.0 A runs for 300 s. What charge passes?
A: \(Q=It=(2.0)(300)=600\text{ C}\).
