Unit 7: Equilibrium

Big Picture

Equilibrium is a dynamic balance, not a stop. Use \(Q\) versus \(K\) as your decision tool before doing heavy algebra.

7.1 Introduction to Equilibrium

▼
  • Dynamic equilibrium means forward and reverse rates are equal.

7.2 Direction of Reversible Reactions

▼
  • Compare Q to K to predict direction of shift.

7.3 Reaction Quotient and Equilibrium Constant

▼
\(Q \lt K:\ \text{shifts right},\quad Q \gt K:\ \text{shifts left}\)

7.4 Calculating the Equilibrium Constant

▼
\(\mathrm{aA+bB \rightleftharpoons cC+dD,\ K_c=\frac{[C]^c[D]^d}{[A]^a[B]^b}}\)

7.5 Magnitude of Equilibrium Constant

▼
  • K >> 1 indicates product-favored equilibrium.
  • K << 1 indicates reactant-favored equilibrium.

7.6 Properties of Equilibrium Constant

▼
  • Reverse reaction: K_new = 1/K.
  • Multiply coefficients by n: K_new = K^n.

7.7 Calculating Equilibrium Concentrations

▼
  • RICE tables are standard for setup and solving.
RICE Workflow
  • R: write balanced reaction and K expression.
  • I: list initial concentrations.
  • C: assign changes with \(x\).
  • E: write equilibrium expressions and solve.

7.8 Representations of Equilibrium

▼
  • Graphs/tables can show approach to equilibrium and concentration plateaus.

7.9 Introduction to Le Chatelier's Principle

▼
  • Systems shift to reduce imposed stress (concentration, pressure, temperature).
Common Mistake

Adding an inert gas at constant volume does not change partial pressures of reacting gases, so equilibrium position does not shift.

7.10 Reaction Quotient and Le Chatelier's Principle

▼
  • Concentration disturbances can be interpreted via immediate Q vs K change.

7.11 Introduction to Solubility Equilibria

▼
  • Sparingly soluble salts establish equilibrium with dissolved ions.
  • Ksp expression uses ion concentrations raised to stoichiometric powers.
