How To Study This Unit

Move topic by topic and connect ideas across representations. For every problem, explain what the quantity means before computing it. This improves both speed and accuracy on timed AP multiple-choice and free-response work.

Use a three-pass routine: concept check, worked examples, then timed mixed practice. Keep an error log by topic ID to detect weak patterns quickly.

7.1 Modeling Situations with Differential Equations

▼

This section builds exam-ready habits: identify the representation (graph/table/formula/context), pick the theorem or rule, then justify each major step. Keep your writing concise but explicit, because AP rubric points are earned by correct reasoning and interpretation, not only final answers.

\(f'(x)=\lim_{h\to0}\frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h}\), and interpret units as \(\frac{\text{output}}{\text{input}}\).
How To Solve
  • Start with known quantities, target quantity, and units.
  • Choose the procedure that matches the structure of the problem.
  • Check domain conditions/endpoints and state a brief interpretation sentence.
Common Mistake

Skipping justification language (for example, writing an answer without naming the theorem or condition). On AP FRQs, that often loses communication points even when algebra is correct.

AP-Style Prompt
Given \(dy/dx=f(x,y)\), sketch slope behavior and estimate \(y\) after one Euler step.
