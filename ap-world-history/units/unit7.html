Unit 7: Global Conflict (c. 1900-present)

← AP World History
From-Scratch Overview

Unit 7 examines why global wars occurred, how states mobilized societies for total war, and how conflict reshaped global order.

Use layered causation: long-term imperial rivalry and nationalism combined with short-term crises and alliance commitments.

The exam rewards students who connect battlefront change to civilian and colonial experiences.

Detailed Core Notes

▼

World War I emerged from militarization, alliance blocs, imperial competition, and nationalist volatility.

Total war expanded state control over industry, labor, information, and civilian life.

Interwar instability, economic crisis, and authoritarian expansion contributed to World War II.

Mass violence and genocide, including the Holocaust, transformed international legal norms and memory politics.

Colonial troops and resources made both wars global, not purely European conflicts.

Postwar settlements reorganized institutions, boundaries, and legitimacy frameworks.

The Cold War inherited unresolved tensions while creating new ideological bipolarity and proxy warfare.

AP responses should compare continuity and change between World War I and World War II mobilization patterns.

Deeper Explanation and Analysis

▼

Evidence clusters include trench warfare and home fronts, fascist expansion, genocide policy, and postwar institutions.

Strong conclusions emphasize that twentieth-century war transformed both state capacity and global political imagination.

Do not isolate Europe; include Asia-Pacific and colonial theaters in global conflict narratives.

Civilian targeting and ideological warfare are central analytical categories, not side topics.

Discuss how war accelerated decolonization by weakening empires and legitimizing anti-colonial claims.

High-scoring conclusions identify both immediate outcomes and long-term structural shifts.

Practice and Exam Explanation

▼

DBQ grouping: causes, wartime methods, and postwar consequences.

Contextualization move: link pre-1914 imperial competition to later total-war escalation.

Avoid presentist language; keep claims anchored to period evidence.

Use map evidence to reinforce global scope rather than single-region narratives.

How To Use This In FRQs
  • Open with a claim that uses extent language (high, moderate, limited) rather than vague wording.
  • Build each body paragraph around one analytical category and two specific examples.
  • Use one sentence per paragraph to explain why your evidence matters for the prompt.
  • Conclude by weighing limits or counterevidence to show complexity.
SAQ Practice
Identify one specific development from this unit and explain one significant effect with direct evidence.
DBQ Guidance
Group documents by analytical category first, then add one external fact not directly stated in the set.
LEQ Prompt
Evaluate the extent to which World War II changed global power structures by 1950.
Common Mistake To Avoid

Do not summarize events without argument. Every paragraph should make and defend a claim tied to the prompt.
