Unit 8: Cold War and Decolonization (c. 1900-present)

← AP World History
From-Scratch Overview

Unit 8 connects superpower rivalry with decolonization movements and postcolonial state-building challenges.

Treat decolonization as varied pathways rather than one model: negotiated transfer, mass civil resistance, and armed struggle.

Your AP writing should balance structural Cold War constraints with regional agency.

Detailed Core Notes

▼

The United States and Soviet Union advanced competing ideological and development frameworks.

Proxy wars linked local struggles to global strategic rivalry in Korea, Vietnam, Afghanistan, and beyond.

Nuclear deterrence constrained direct superpower war but intensified arms races and intelligence operations.

Independence movements in India, Algeria, Ghana, Indonesia, and elsewhere show diverse political strategies.

New states confronted borders inherited from empire, economic dependency, and legitimacy challenges.

Nonalignment attempted autonomy from superpower blocs while navigating material pressures.

Postcolonial governance varied widely; some states stabilized institutions while others faced coups or civil conflict.

In AP essays, compare how global Cold War conditions shaped but did not fully determine local outcomes.

Deeper Explanation and Analysis

▼

Evidence clusters include Bandung, nonaligned diplomacy, anti-colonial leaders, and proxy-conflict regional effects.

A strong conclusion balances structural constraints with local political agency in decolonization trajectories.

Avoid monocausal claims that the Cold War alone determined decolonization outcomes.

Compare local leadership choices, social coalition structures, and post-independence institutional capacity.

A strong essay explains why similarly constrained states produced different trajectories.

Use periodization carefully: immediate independence politics can differ sharply from later governance outcomes.

Practice and Exam Explanation

▼

DBQ grouping: superpower strategy, nationalist leadership, and state-building constraints.

Complexity move: compare one negotiated independence case with one violent decolonization case.

Use specific place names and leaders to ground abstract claims.

Include both diplomatic and social evidence for top-scoring analysis.

How To Use This In FRQs
  • Open with a claim that uses extent language (high, moderate, limited) rather than vague wording.
  • Build each body paragraph around one analytical category and two specific examples.
  • Use one sentence per paragraph to explain why your evidence matters for the prompt.
  • Conclude by weighing limits or counterevidence to show complexity.
SAQ Practice
Identify one specific development from this unit and explain one significant effect with direct evidence.
DBQ Guidance
Group documents by analytical category first, then add one external fact not directly stated in the set.
LEQ Prompt
Evaluate the extent to which Cold War rivalry influenced decolonization outcomes after 1945.
Common Mistake To Avoid

Do not summarize events without argument. Every paragraph should make and defend a claim tied to the prompt.
