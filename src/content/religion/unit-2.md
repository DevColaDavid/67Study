## Quick Reference — Four Gospels

| Gospel | Written For | Key Theme | Unique Features |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Matthew** | Jewish audience | Jesus as teacher & fulfillment of OT prophecy | Begins with genealogy; Sermon on the Mount; Great Commission |
| **Mark** | Gentile (Roman) audience | Jesus as servant; action-focused | Shortest Gospel; written first (~65-70 AD) |
| **Luke** | Theophilus / Gentiles | Jesus' compassion for the poor and marginalized | Written by a physician; includes Prodigal Son, Good Samaritan |
| **John** | All believers | Jesus as the divine Word (Logos) | 7 signs/miracles; "I Am" statements; written last |

> [!tip] Synoptics vs. John
> Matthew, Mark, and Luke are called **Synoptic Gospels** ("seen together") because they share a similar structure and many of the same stories. John stands apart with a unique theological focus.
