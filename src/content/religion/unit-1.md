# Unit 1: Core Theology & Church

## Just War — 2 Situations the Church Accepts

The Catholic Church teaches that war is generally sinful, but recognizes it may be morally permitted under strict conditions (Catechism of the Catholic Church #2309):

1. **Self-Defense against an unjust aggressor** — If a nation faces a real, serious, and certain armed attack, it has the right to defend itself, but only after all peaceful alternatives (diplomacy, negotiation) have been exhausted. The damage inflicted by the aggressor must be lasting, grave, and certain.

2. **Protecting innocent civilians from grave injustice** — If a government is massacring or severely oppressing its own people or others, another nation may intervene militarily to protect innocent lives — provided the military response is proportional and there is a reasonable chance of success.

> [!note] Just War Conditions
> Declared by proper authority, just cause, right intention, last resort, proportionality, and reasonable chance of success.

## Miracles of Jesus — 4 Key Miracles

| Miracle | What Happened | Significance |
|---|---|---|
| **Water into Wine** (John 2:1-11) | At a wedding in Cana, Jesus turned 6 stone jars of water into fine wine when the hosts ran out. | His *first* miracle; revealed his divine glory and his care for people's ordinary needs. |
| **Feeding the 5,000** (Mark 6:30-44) | With only 5 loaves and 2 fish, Jesus fed a crowd of 5,000+ with 12 baskets of leftovers. | Shows Jesus as provider of physical and spiritual nourishment; foreshadows the Eucharist. |
| **Walking on Water** (Matthew 14:22-33) | Jesus walked across the Sea of Galilee during a storm. Peter tried but began sinking from fear; Jesus saved him. | Demonstrates Jesus' mastery over nature; calls us to trust him even in life's storms. |
| **Raising Lazarus** (John 11:1-44) | Lazarus had been dead 4 days. Jesus wept, then called out "Lazarus, come forth!" — and he walked out alive. | Jesus declares "I am the resurrection and the life." Greatest miracle before his own resurrection. |

## Parables — 2 Key Parables

### The Good Samaritan (Luke 10:25-37)

**Story:** A Jewish man was beaten, robbed, and left for dead on the road to Jericho. A priest and a Levite both passed by and avoided him. A Samaritan — a group despised by Jews — stopped, treated his wounds, took him to an inn, and paid for his care.

**Messages:**
- Love your neighbor unconditionally — your "neighbor" is anyone in need, regardless of race, religion, or social status.
- True faith is shown through compassionate action, not just religious status.
- Challenges prejudice and calls us to see the dignity in every person.

### The Prodigal Son (Luke 15:11-32)

**Story:** A younger son demands his inheritance early, leaves, and wastes everything. Starving and desperate, he returns home to beg to be a servant. His father sees him from a distance, runs to him, and throws a lavish celebration. The older son who stayed is bitter. The father says: *"This son of mine was dead and is alive again; he was lost and is found."*

**Messages:**
- God's love is unconditional — no matter how far we stray, God is always ready to welcome us back.
- Repentance and humility open the door to God's forgiveness and grace.
- We are called not to be judgmental of others who are forgiven, but to rejoice in their return.

## Blessed Joseph William Chaminade

| Question | Answer |
|---|---|
| **Where did he escape from?** | Escaped from **Bordeaux, France** during the French Revolution (1792), when the revolutionary government was persecuting Catholic clergy. He fled to **Zaragoza, Spain**. |
| **What statue inspired him?** | The statue of **Our Lady of the Pillar** (*Nuestra Señora del Pilar*) in Zaragoza. He prayed deeply on Mary's role in rebuilding the Church. |
| **What did he found?** | Inspired by Mary, he returned to France and founded the **Society of Mary (Marianists)** — officially approved in 1817. Mission: re-Christianize France through Marian spirituality and education. |

## Order of the Mass — All 4 Parts

### 1) Introductory Rite
- Sign of the Cross and Greeting — *"The Lord be with you / And with your spirit"*
- Penitential Act (Confiteor) — We acknowledge our sinfulness
- Gloria — Hymn of praise (except Advent & Lent)
- Collect (Opening Prayer)

### 2) Liturgy of the Word
- First Reading — Usually from the Old Testament
- Responsorial Psalm
- Second Reading — From the NT Epistles
- Gospel Acclamation (Alleluia)
- Gospel Reading — From one of the four Gospels
- Homily — Priest/deacon explains and applies Scripture
- Creed (Nicene Creed) — We profess our faith
- Universal Prayer (Prayer of the Faithful)

### 3) Liturgy of the Eucharist
- Preparation of the Gifts (Offertory) — Bread, wine, and offerings brought to the altar
- Eucharistic Prayer — Central prayer of the Mass
- Consecration — *"This is my Body… This is my Blood"* — transubstantiation occurs
- Memorial Acclamation — *"We proclaim your Death, O Lord…"*
- Lord's Prayer (Our Father)
- Sign of Peace
- Breaking of the Bread (Agnus Dei / Lamb of God)
- Holy Communion

### 4) Concluding Rite
- Final Blessing — Priest blesses assembly in the name of the Trinity
- Dismissal — *"Go in peace, glorifying the Lord by your life"*
- Sends us forth to live the Gospel in our daily lives

## Stations of the Cross — #4, 5, 6

| Station | Title | What Happened | How It Shows Love |
|---|---|---|---|
| **#4** | Jesus Meets His Sorrowful Mother Mary | Along the Via Dolorosa, Jesus encounters Mary in the crowd. Their eyes meet in deep sorrow and love. | Mary's presence despite her own immense grief shows a mother's unconditional love — she refuses to abandon her son. |
| **#5** | Simon of Cyrene Helps Jesus Carry the Cross | Roman soldiers pulled Simon from the crowd to help carry the heavy cross when Jesus could no longer bear it. | Simon literally shared in Jesus' suffering, modeling what it means to carry one another's burdens. |
| **#6** | Veronica Wipes the Face of Jesus | Veronica pushed through the crowd and used her veil to wipe the blood, sweat, and dirt from Jesus' face. Tradition holds that Jesus' image was miraculously imprinted on the cloth. | A simple, courageous act of compassion and dignity toward a suffering person. |

## Movie Reflection — *The Way* (2010)

*The Way* follows **Tom**, an American doctor who travels to Spain to retrieve the remains of his son Daniel, who died on the first day of walking the **Camino de Santiago** — the ancient Christian pilgrimage route. Instead of going home, Tom walks the entire 800km Camino himself, carrying his son's ashes, to honor his life.

**Key themes:**

- **Grief and letting go:** Tom is emotionally closed off and hadn't truly connected with his son. The Camino forces him to grieve, open up, and heal.

- **Walking in faith without all the answers:** Tom doesn't know why he's walking — he just walks. Sometimes in life, we move forward in faith without a clear destination.

- **Community and unexpected friendships:** Tom meets three strangers all walking for different reasons. Together they become a family.

- **The life we live vs. the life we choose:** Daniel tells his father, *"You don't choose a life, Dad. You live one."* — a call to be present, bold, and intentional.

- **Pilgrimage as a metaphor for faith:** The Camino reflects the Christian life — long, difficult, beautiful, and walked one step at a time. We carry our crosses and find grace along the way.
