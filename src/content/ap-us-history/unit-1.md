# Period 1 — A New World (1491–1607)

---

## 1.2 — Native American Societies Before European Contact

### Early Migration and Settlement
- First inhabitants migrated from Asia across a **land bridge** (Siberia → Alaska) at least 10,000–40,000 years ago.
- Migrants spread from the Arctic Circle to the tip of South America, evolving into hundreds of tribes with unique languages.

### Central and South American Civilizations

| Civilization | Region | Key Traits |
|---|---|---|
| **Mayas** (300–800 AD) | Yucatán Peninsula (Guatemala, Belize, Mexico) | Rainforest cities, advanced calendar |
| **Aztecs** | Central Mexico; capital **Tenochtitlán** (~200,000 people) | Powerful empire, corn cultivation |
| **Incas** | Peru, western South America | Vast empire, potato cultivation |

- **Common Traits:** Highly organized societies, extensive trade networks, advanced calendars, stable food supplies.

### North American Regional Cultures

| Region | Groups / Notes |
|---|---|
| **Southwest** | Hohokam, Anasazi, Pueblos — cliff dwellings, maize irrigation |
| **Northwest** | Pacific coast — longhouses, fishing, carved totem poles |
| **Great Plains** | Nomadic buffalo hunters, teepees; some riverine farming |
| **Mississippi Valley** | Woodland peoples — mound builders; **Cahokia** (~30,000 people near East St. Louis) |
| **Northeast** | Adena-Hopewell descendants; **Iroquois Confederation** (Seneca, Cayuga, Onondaga, Oneida, Mohawk, Tuscarora) |
| **Atlantic Seaboard** | Cherokee, Lumbee — timber lodgings, river/ocean fishing |

## 1.3 — European Exploration in the Americas

### Why Europeans Came

1. **Changes in Thought & Technology** — Renaissance curiosity, printing press (1450s), improved shipbuilding, compass, gunpowder.
2. **Religious Conflict** — Reconquista (1492), Protestant Reformation → spread of Christianity.
3. **Expanding Trade** — Ottoman control of Constantinople (1453) blocked overland routes; Portuguese opened sea routes around Africa (Vasco da Gama → India, 1498).
4. **Slave Trading** — Portuguese began West African slave trade in the 15th century; sugar plantation model created.

> Just remember: **God, Glory, and Gold.**

### Dividing the Americas

| Agreement | Year | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| **Papal Line of Demarcation** | 1493 | Spain (west) / Portugal (east) |
| **Treaty of Tordesillas** | 1494 | Portugal gets Brazil; Spain gets the rest |

- **English Claims:** John Cabot (1497) explored Newfoundland; Sir Francis Drake raided Spanish ships; Roanoke colony failed (1587).
- **French Claims:** Verrazzano (1524) explored eastern coast; Cartier (1534–1542) explored the St. Lawrence River.

## 1.4 — The Columbian Exchange, Spanish Exploration, and Conquest

### Christopher Columbus
- Gained support from **Isabella and Ferdinand** of Spain in 1492.
- Landed in the **Bahamas, October 12, 1492** → triggered sustained European contact with the Americas.

### The Columbian Exchange
The widespread transfer of plants, animals, people, diseases, and ideas between the Americas and the Old World.

| Direction | Transferred |
|---|---|
| **Americas → Europe** | Corn, potatoes, tomatoes, tobacco, beans; syphilis |
| **Europe → Americas** | Sugar cane, horses, pigs, wheels, iron tools, guns, **smallpox & measles** |

> **Impact:** Mexico's population collapsed from ~22 million (1492) to ~4 million by mid-16th century due to disease.

### Economic Changes in Europe
- **Decline of Feudalism → Rise of Capitalism**: Control of capital (money & machinery) overtook land ownership.
- **Joint-Stock Companies**: Spread financial risk of ocean voyages among many investors → encouraged exploration.

### Spanish Conquest

| Conquistador | Achievement |
|---|---|
| **Balboa** | Crossed Panama to the Pacific |
| **Magellan** | First circumnavigation of the globe |
| **Hernán Cortés** | Conquered the Aztecs in Mexico |
| **Francisco Pizarro** | Conquered the Incas in Peru |

## 1.5 — Labor, Slavery, and Caste in the Spanish Colonial System

### Labor Systems

- **Encomienda System**: Spanish colonists given control over Native populations for labor in agriculture & mining (supposed to provide care/Christian instruction in return).
- **Asiento System**: Spanish imported enslaved Africans, taxed per individual. Between **10–15 million Africans** were forcibly brought to the Americas (significant mortality during the *Middle Passage*).

### African Resistance
- Escape, sabotage, revolts, and maintaining cultural heritage (music, religion, community).

### Spanish Caste System

| Level | Group |
|---|---|
| Top | Pure-blooded Spaniards |
| Middle | Mixed-heritage individuals |
| Bottom | Pure Native American or African heritage |

> The caste system entrenched racial discrimination and social stratification throughout the colonies.

## 1.6 — Cultural Interactions Between Europeans, Native Americans, and Africans

### European Policies Toward Native Americans

| Power | Approach |
|---|---|
| **Spanish** | Subjugation via encomienda; Bartolomé de Las Casas advocated reform → *New Laws of 1542*; **Valladolid Debate** (Las Casas vs. Sepúlveda) |
| **English** | Initial trade/peaceful relations → land conflicts and displacement as population grew |
| **French** | Alliances & fur trade; minimal colonial imposition; best relations with Native Americans |

### Native American Strategies
- Allied with Europeans against rival tribes.
- Migrated westward to avoid conflict.
- Fragmented tribal identities hindered unified resistance.

### African Cultural Influence
- Introduced agricultural knowledge, music (e.g., the banjo).
- Europeans rationalized slavery through biblical interpretations and racial theories.
