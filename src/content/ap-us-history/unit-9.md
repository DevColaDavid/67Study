# Period 9 — Global Role (1980–Present)

---

## 9.2 — Reagan and Conservatism

### Reagan's Rise
- Former actor and California governor; prominent conservative figure.
- Gained national attention in **Goldwater's 1964 campaign**; nearly secured 1976 Republican nomination.
- Won presidency in **1980**; Iranian hostages released on inauguration day.
- Survived assassination attempt early in presidency → boosted popularity.

### The Reagan Revolution — Goals
1. Reduce size of federal government.
2. Lower taxes.
3. Cut welfare spending.
4. Strengthen the military.
5. Create a more conservative federal judiciary.

### Reaganomics (Supply-Side Economics)
- Tax cuts and reduced government spending → stimulate private investment and economic growth.
- Critics called it **"trickle-down economics"** — benefited the wealthy with hope prosperity would filter down.

### Federal Tax Reduction
- **Economic Recovery Tax Act (1981):** 25% personal income tax cut over 3 years; reduced corporate and capital gains taxes.

### Spending
- Domestic program cuts >$40 billion (food stamps, student loans, mass transportation).
- **Military spending increased dramatically.**
- Bipartisan bill strengthened Social Security (raised retirement age, taxed some benefits).

### Deregulation
- Eased restrictions on savings and loans, corporate mergers, environmental protections, auto emissions.
- Interior Secretary James Watt — opened federal lands for coal, timber, offshore oil.

### Labor
- Fired striking air traffic controllers (PATCO) → decertified union.
- Union membership: 30%+ (1962) → ~12% (late 1990s).

### Recession and Recovery
- **1982 Recession:** Bank failures; 11% unemployment.
- **1983 Recovery:** Inflation below 4%; economy rebounded; income gap widened.

### Budget and Trade Deficits
- National debt tripled: $900B → $2.7 trillion.
- Trade deficit grew to $150B annually.
- **Gramm-Rudman-Hollings Act** — mandated spending cuts and tax increases.

### 1988 Election
- **George H.W. Bush (R):** Promised "Read my lips — no new taxes."
- **Michael Dukakis (D):** Portrayed as soft on crime and defense.
- Bush won.

### Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA, 1990)
- Prohibited discrimination against citizens with physical and mental disabilities in hiring, transportation, and public accommodation.

### Civil Rights Developments

| Group | Key Events |
|---|---|
| **Women** | Increased workforce participation; pay gap narrowed; #MeToo movement (2017) exposed sexual harassment |
| **African Americans** | Rodney King beating (1991); acquittal sparked national conversation; Black Lives Matter (2013); George Floyd's death (2020) |
| **LGBT** | Clinton's "don't ask, don't tell" (military); hate crimes law (2009); **Obergefell v. Hodges (2015)** — legalized same-sex marriage nationwide (14th Amendment) |

## 9.3 — The End of the Cold War

### Reagan's Military Buildup
- Increased defense spending: $171B (1981) → $300B+ (1985).
- New weapons: B-1 bomber, MX missile.
- **Strategic Defense Initiative (SDI / "Star Wars"):** Missile defense via lasers and particle beams; critics worried it would escalate arms race.

### Improved U.S.-Soviet Relations
- **Mikhail Gorbachev (Soviet leader, 1985):**
  - **Glasnost:** Political openness.
  - **Perestroika:** Economic restructuring (free-market practices).
  - Sought to end costly arms race.
- **Reagan at Berlin Wall (1987):** "Mr. Gorbachev, tear down this wall."

### Summit Meetings

| Summit | Year | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| Geneva | 1985 | Cultural/scientific/environmental agreements |
| Reykjavik | 1986 | Less successful; disagreements on missile defense |
| Washington, D.C. | 1987 | **INF Agreement** — destroyed intermediate-range missiles |

### Collapse of Soviet Communism
- Nationalist movements overwhelmed Gorbachev.
- Baltic states declared independence (1990).
- **Failed coup (1991)** → Soviet Union dissolved **December 1991**.
- **Boris Yeltsin** formed **Commonwealth of Independent States (CIS)** with 9 former Soviet republics.
- Yeltsin disbanded Communist Party; began democracy and free-market economy.

### End of the Cold War (Officially)
- **START I (1991):** Bush and Gorbachev — nuclear warheads reduced to under 10,000 each.
- **START II (1992):** Bush and Yeltsin — further reduced to ~3,000 each.

### Post-Cold War Europe
- **EU (2002):** Unified market of 15 nations; 12 adopted the euro.
- By 2007: EU expanded to 27 nations, including 10 former Soviet satellites.

## 9.4 — A Changing Economy

### Election of 1992
- **Bill Clinton (D):** Moderate "New Democrat"; focused on economy, jobs, education, health care.
- Defeated Bush (R) despite third-party challenge from Ross Perot; 370 electoral votes, 43% popular vote.

### Clinton's Economic Record
- Achieved **federal surplus** by 1998 — first since 1969.
- Balanced budget preserved Medicare and Social Security; reformed welfare (5-year limit); raised minimum wage.
- Reelected 1996 — first Democrat since FDR to win a second term.

### Clinton's Impeachment
- Lied under oath about sexual relations → **impeached by Republican House (1999)**.
- Not convicted by Senate.

### Economic Expansion
- Longest peacetime economic expansion; growth rates >4% annually.
- Technology drove productivity: Internet, personal computers, wireless communications.
- High-tech companies: Apple, Intel, Microsoft, Amazon, AOL, Yahoo, Google.

### Globalization
- **WTO, IMF, World Bank** — global economic organizations.
- **NAFTA** — free-trade zone with Canada and Mexico.
- G8 controlled 2/3 of global wealth; China, India, Brazil began outpacing older industrial powers.

### Wealth Inequality
- Top 1% owned 35% of nation's wealth by 2007.
- Top 1% held 43% of financial wealth by late 2010s.
- Scholars linked decline of unions to rising inequality.

## 9.5 — Migration and Immigration in the 1990s and 2000s

### Immigration and Nationality Act of 1965 (Legacy)
- Ended racially based quota system; prioritized U.S. relatives and skilled workers.
- Opened immigration to non-European countries.

### Immigration Shifts (1970–2015)

| Group | Change |
|---|---|
| European/Canadian | 68% → 14% |
| South/East Asian | 7% → 27% |
| Mexican | 8% → 27% |
| Latin American (non-Mexico) | 11% → 24% |

### Undocumented Immigration
- **Immigration Reform and Control Act (1986):** Penalized employers; granted amnesty to 3M+.
- **Illegal Immigration Reform and Immigrant Responsibility Act (1996):** Strengthened enforcement; limited success.
- **DACA (2012, Obama):** Protected "Dreamers" from deportation; faced legal challenges.

### Trump's Immigration Policies
- Proposed border wall; ending DACA; restricting legal immigration.
- Reduced migrants by 2019; sparked major debate.

## 9.6 — Challenges of the 21st Century

### Disputed Election of 2000
- Al Gore won popular vote by 500,000+; election hinged on Florida's 25 electoral votes.
- **Bush v. Gore:** Supreme Court halted recount 5–4 → **George W. Bush** won with 271 electoral votes.

### War on Terrorism (Post-9/11)

| Event | Details |
|---|---|
| **September 11, 2001** | Al-Qaeda attacks killed ~3,000; united country against terrorism |
| **Afghanistan** | Bush ordered overthrow of Taliban; succeeded in toppling regime but Afghanistan remained unstable |
| **USA PATRIOT Act** | Enhanced government surveillance and investigative powers |
| **Dept. of Homeland Security** | Largest government reorganization since WWII |

### Iraq War
- Bush labeled Iraq, N. Korea, Iran the "**axis of evil**" (2002).
- Claimed Iraq was developing WMDs (no evidence linking to 9/11).
- **Operation Iraqi Freedom (March 19, 2003):** Air attacks without UN approval; U.S. quickly overran Iraq.
- **No WMDs found**; Bush declared victory → massive insurgent attacks; prolonged conflict.

### Domestic Policy: George W. Bush (2001–2009)
- **No Child Left Behind Act** — increased educational accountability and testing.
- **Medicare reform** — prescription drug coverage.
- **Hurricane Katrina (2005):** FEMA failure → loss of public confidence → Democrats gained Congress in 2006.

### Election of 2008
- Long Democratic primary: Hillary Clinton vs. **Barack Obama**.
- Obama chose Joe Biden as VP; Republicans: John McCain / Sarah Palin.
- Obama won: 364 electoral votes; **first African American president**.
- Faced economic crisis and ongoing wars.

### Obama's Foreign Policy
- Increased troops in Afghanistan; used drone attacks in Pakistan.
- **Osama bin Laden killed (May 2011):** CIA and Navy SEAL operation in Pakistan.
- **ISIS** — extremist group in Iraq/Syria seeking global caliphate; Obama provided air support and military training.

### Domestic Policy: Obama (2009–2017)
- **Economic Stimulus (Keynesian):** Response to Great Recession.
- **Dodd-Frank Act (2010):** Financial regulation reform; prevented future crises.
- **Affordable Care Act (2010, "Obamacare"):** Expanded health insurance; individual mandate; Medicaid expansion; subsidies.
- Obama won reelection 2012 — 332 to 206 electoral votes; strong Hispanic support.

### Election of 2016
- **Donald Trump (R):** Real estate developer/reality TV star; "Make America Great Again"; attacked immigration and trade deals; won key swing states; **306 electoral votes despite losing popular vote by 3 million**.
- **Hillary Clinton (D):** First woman nominated for president by a major party.
- Republicans maintained control of House and Senate.

### 2020 Pandemic (COVID-19)
- Identified in China (Dec 2019); confirmed in U.S. (Jan 2020).
- Trump administration's inconsistent response; 500,000+ American deaths by early 2021.
- **Operation Warp Speed** — accelerated vaccine development; first vaccinations December 2020.

### Election of 2020
- Increased mail-in voting due to pandemic.
- **Joe Biden (D)** — Obama's VP; chose **Kamala Harris** (first person of color nominated for VP).
- Biden won by 7 million votes; **306 electoral votes**.
- Trump falsely claimed victory; ongoing disputes.

### Attack on the Capitol (January 6, 2021)
- Trump rally led to supporters storming the U.S. Capitol.
- First such attack since 1814; 5 deaths.
- Senate confirmed Biden's victory.
- Trump became **first president impeached twice**; not convicted.

### Biden's Administration
- Called for unity in inaugural address.
- **$1.9 trillion pandemic relief package.**
- Plan to vaccinate all Americans by fall 2021.
