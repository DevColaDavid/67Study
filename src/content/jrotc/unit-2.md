# Unit 2: Skills, Drill & Knowledge

## Leadership

### Definitions

> **Leadership** — The ability to influence subordinates in order to accomplish a mission.

> **Teamwork** — The cooperative effort of a group of people working together to achieve a common goal. The key to success in JROTC.

### Three Leadership Styles

| Style | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Delegating** | Giving decision-making authority to subordinates |
| **Directing** | Instructing subordinates |
| **Participating** | Involving subordinates in the decision-making process |

### Three Stages of Team Building

| Stage | Description |
|---|---|
| **Formation Stage** | Team comes together; members get to know each other |
| **Enrichment Stage** | Team develops skills and cohesion |
| **Sustainment Stage** | Team operates at peak performance |

### Key Rules

| Question | Answer |
|---|---|
| Two parts of a command? | Preparatory command and command of execution |

### Drill Commands

| Command | Action |
|---|---|
| **Fall In** | Form up in assigned position |
| **Fall Out** | Dismissed from formation (informal) |
| **Attention** | Heels together, thumbs along trouser seams, eyes front |
| **Parade Rest** | Feet shoulder-width, hands clasped behind back, eyes front |
| **At Ease** | Left foot stays; may move but not talk |
| **Rest** | Left foot stays; may move and talk |
| **Right Face** | Pivot 90° clockwise |
| **Left Face** | Pivot 90° counterclockwise |
| **About Face** | Pivot 180° to the rear |
| **Forward March** | Step off with left foot |
| **Halt** | Stop on either foot |

## Map Reading

> Always read **RIGHT then UP** — *"Read right, then up"*

### Map Colors

| Color | Represents |
|---|---|
| **Blue** | Water |
| **Green** | Vegetation |
| **Brown** | Contour lines, elevation, and relief |
| **Black** | Man-made objects |
| **Red** | Highly populated areas and major roads |

### Three Types of North

| Type | Definition |
|---|---|
| **True North** | Direction to the geographic North Pole |
| **Magnetic North** | Direction a compass needle points |
| **Grid North** | Direction of vertical grid lines on a map |

### Five Major Terrain Features

| Feature | Description |
|---|---|
| **Hill** | Closed contour circles; elevation increases inward |
| **Saddle** | Low point or dip between two hills |
| **Valley** | U or V-shape contours pointing uphill; water flows through |
| **Ridge** | Elongated high ground; contours point downhill |
| **Depression** | Closed contours with hachure marks (ticks pointing inward) |

> [!tip] Memory Trick
> **"He Saw Very Rough Dirt"** = Hill, Saddle, Valley, Ridge, Depression

### Contour Lines

- Connect points of **equal elevation**
- **Closer = steeper** | **Wider = flatter**
- **Index contour** = every 5th line, darker and labeled
- **Contour interval** = vertical distance between lines (check the map margin)

### Grid Coordinates (MGRS)

| Grid | Accuracy |
|---|---|
| 4-digit | 1,000 m |
| 6-digit | 100 m |
| 8-digit | 10 m |

### Azimuth & Compass

- Full circle = **360°** | N = 0°/360°, E = 90°, S = 180°, W = 270°
- **Back azimuth:** < 180° → add 180° | > 180° → subtract 180°

### Map Scale

| Scale | 1 cm = | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| 1:25,000 | 250 m | Most detail |
| 1:50,000 | 500 m | Most common military map |
| 1:250,000 | 2.5 km | Least detail |

## Rope Knots

| Knot | Use | Key Tip |
|---|---|---|
| **Square Knot** | Join two ropes of the same diameter | Right over left, left over right |
| **Bowline** | Fixed loop that won't slip; rescue/safety | "Rabbit out of hole, around tree, back in hole" |
| **Clove Hitch** | Secure rope to a post or pole; quick & adjustable | Two loops, second behind first, tuck under |
| **Two Half Hitches** | Tie rope to a fixed object | Two half hitches in the same direction |
| **Figure-Eight** | Stopper; climbing and rappelling | Loop → wrap → pass through; makes the #8 shape |
| **Lark's Head** | Attach rope to a ring or bar | Fold in half → loop over bar → pass ends through |

> [!warning] Always dress and set your knots — a loose knot is a dangerous knot!

## First Aid and Health

> **First Aid** — The immediate care given to an injured or ill victim before professional medical help arrives.

### Two Types of Fractures

| Type | Also Called |
|---|---|
| Open | Compound fracture |
| Closed | Simple fracture |

### Four Life-Saving Steps

1. Check for **consciousness**
2. Check for **breathing and heartbeat**
3. Check for **bleeding**
4. Check for **shock**
