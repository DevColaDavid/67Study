# Unit 1: The Global Tapestry (1200–1450 CE)

## 🧠 Core Idea

The world from 1200–1450 contained a rich diversity of states and empires — in China, the Middle East, South/Southeast Asia, the Americas, Africa, and Europe — each using **religion, bureaucracy, military power, and trade** to build and maintain authority. No region was isolated; cultural traditions spread and influenced neighbors.

---

## ⚡ Key Takeaways

- The Song Dynasty used Neo-Confucianism and the Civil Service Exam to legitimize rule — a **continuity** from earlier Chinese dynasties.
- Champa rice from Vietnam (via tribute system) caused a **population explosion** in China.
- The Abbasid Caliphate fragmented by 1200; **Turkic Muslim states** (Seljuks, Delhi Sultanate, Mamluks) filled the vacuum.
- Dar al-Islam preserved Greek/Roman knowledge in the House of Wisdom, later transferring it back to Europe.
- South and Southeast Asia had **three major faiths** competing: Hinduism, Buddhism, Islam.
- The Americas produced two very different imperial models: **centralized Inca** vs. **decentralized Aztec**.
- Africa showed diversity — from centralized Mali to decentralized Hausa city-states.
- Europe was politically decentralized under **feudalism** and **manorialism**, unified only by the Catholic Church.

---

## 📑 Section Breakdown

### 🐉 China: The Song Dynasty (960–1276 CE)

- **State legitimacy**: Revival of Confucianism as Neo-Confucianism — ancient, therefore authoritative.
- **Civil Service Exam**: Based on Confucian classics. Began under the Han Dynasty — a **continuity**.
- **Confucian social order**: Hierarchical — state > citizens, men > women. **Filial piety** = children must honor parents and ancestors.
- **Women in Song China**: Subordinate, could not own property or remarry. **Footbinding** became popular in elite circles.
- **Economy**: Flourished through silk, porcelain, iron/steel production; commercialization; expansion of the Grand Canal.
- **Champa Rice**: Drought-resistant, early-maturing rice from Vietnam via tribute system → population growth.
- **Cultural influence on neighbors**: Korea, Japan, Vietnam adopted Confucianism, filial piety, and Mahayana Buddhism via Tribute System.

**Buddhism basics**:
- **Four Noble Truths**: Suffering exists; desire causes it; stop desire → stop suffering; follow the Eightfold Path.
- **Goal**: End reincarnation cycle → achieve **nirvana**.
- **Theravada**: Original; monks in monasteries.
- **Mahayana**: Spread to East Asia; **bodhisattvas** help others reach nirvana.

---

### ☪️ Middle East: The Abbasid Caliphate (750–1258 CE)

- Islam, Judaism, Christianity: all Abrahamic, all monotheistic. Muhammad = "final prophet."
- **Abbasid decline**: Fell to the Mongols in **1258** when Baghdad was destroyed.
- **Seljuk Empire**: Turkic group that took power from the weakened Abbasids. Also: Delhi Sultanate (South Asia), Mamluk Sultanate (Egypt).
- **House of Wisdom** in Baghdad preserved and translated Greek philosophers → transferred back to Europe → **Renaissance**.
- **Nasir al-Din al-Tusi** invented trigonometry.
- **Sufis**: Missionaries who allowed some local belief → helped spread Islam.

---

### 🕌 South and Southeast Asia

- **Hinduism**: Dominant in South Asia. **Bhakti Hinduism** challenged gender/social hierarchies.
- **Buddhism**: Reduced to monastic communities by c. 1200.
- **Islam**: Religion of the elite after the **Delhi Sultanate** established.
- **Majapahit Kingdom** (Java): Buddhist; controlled sea trade routes.
- **Khmer Empire**: Started Hindu, adopted Buddhism. **Angkor Wat** reflects both faiths.
- Diasporic merchant communities brought religions to SE Asia; governments adopted them to build trade relationships.

---

### 🌎 State Building in the Americas

- **Aztec**: Founded **1345** by the Mexica; capital **Tenochtitlan**; **decentralized**; controlled via **tribute** and sacrifice.
- **Inca**: Highly **centralized**; **Mit'a system** = labor tax for state projects.
- **Mississippian Culture**: Mississippi River Valley; agricultural; **mound builders** (Cahokia: 80 mounds up to 100 feet tall).

---

### 🌍 State Building in Africa

- **Swahili City-States**: Grew through Indian Ocean Trade; **Swahili language** = Bantu + Arabic.
- **Mali, Ghana, Songhay**: Centralized; grew through Dar al-Islam trade; **Mansa Musa** monopolized trade routes.
- **Hausa Kingdom**: Decentralized city-states; brokered Trans-Saharan trade.
- **Great Zimbabwe**: Wealthy through gold trade; rulers **never adopted Islam** — unique.
- **Ethiopia**: Christian — unique among African states.

---

### ⛪ Developments in Europe

- **Byzantine Empire**: Orthodox Christianity; shared with Kievan Rus by 988.
- **Roman Catholicism**: Bound fragmented Western Europe together.
- **Muslims**: Iberian Peninsula (8th century conquest).
- **Jews**: Persecuted minority throughout Europe.
- **Feudalism**: Political system — lords, vassals, fiefs; military service for land.
- **Manorialism**: Economic system — **serfs** bound to land; work for lord's protection (NOT property, but cannot leave).
