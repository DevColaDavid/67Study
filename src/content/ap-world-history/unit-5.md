# Unit 5: Revolutions (1750–1900 CE)

## 🧠 Core Idea

The Enlightenment gave people intellectual tools to challenge monarchs and empires, sparking a wave of **Atlantic Revolutions** and **nationalist movements**. Simultaneously, the **Industrial Revolution** transformed economies, created new social classes, and generated fierce reactions — from labor unions and Marxism to defensive industrialization in China and the Ottoman Empire.

---

## ⚡ Key Takeaways

- The Enlightenment introduced **natural rights, social contract, popular sovereignty, and democracy** — all challenges to monarchist rule.
- The American, French, Haitian, and Latin American Revolutions were all connected through Enlightenment ideas.
- The **Declaration of Independence**, **Declaration of the Rights of Man**, and **Letter from Jamaica** are the three documents you MUST know. ⭐
- The Haitian Revolution was the **only fully successful large-scale slave rebellion** in history.
- Britain industrialized first due to **7 specific factors** (AP curriculum).
- Industrialization created a new social hierarchy: **industrialists → middle class → working class**.
- **Karl Marx** developed Scientific Socialism as a direct response to working-class suffering.
- Japan's **Meiji Restoration** was the most successful defensive industrialization in the world.

## 📑 Section Breakdown

### 💡 The Enlightenment

**What was it?**: Applied the methods of the Scientific Revolution (rational, empirical thinking) to the human world and politics.

**Key Enlightenment Ideas** (all challenged the status quo):
- **Natural Rights**: All humans are born with inherent rights (life, liberty, property).
- **Social Contract**: Government is an agreement between rulers and ruled. Tyrannical government → can be **overthrown**.
- **Popular Sovereignty**: The power to rule **belongs to the people**.
- **Democracy**: All people can participate in government.
- **Liberalism**: Civil rights, representative government, private property, free-market trade.

**Effects**:
- **Suffrage** expanded after American Revolution.
- **Feminism**: Olympe de Gouges wrote *Declaration of the Rights of Woman and the Female Citizen* — criticized French Constitution for excluding women.
- **Abolition**: Natural rights arguments → abolition of slavery in Americas and serfdom in Russia.

---

### 🌍 Nationalism

**Definition**: Shared identity (language, religion, customs) + shared vision + common enemy.

**How leaders used it**: Start revolutions; add nationalist education to schools; demand linguistic unity.

> [!example] Non-Atlantic Example
> **Muhammad Ali** (Egypt): Frustrated with Ottoman corruption; independently opened textile and weapons factories — discontent with imperial/monarchist rule.

---

### 🗽 The Atlantic Revolutions

#### American Revolution
- **Cause**: 13 colonies unhappy with British rule.
- **Document**: ***Declaration of Independence*** ⭐ — reflects **social contract** and **popular sovereignty**.
- **Effects**: Democratic republic; model and inspiration for other revolutions.

#### French Revolution
- **Document**: ***Declaration of the Rights of Man and of the Citizen*** ⭐ — rights of all French men; republican government; government transparency.

#### Haitian Revolution
- **Cause**: Enslaved population in French colony Haiti learned of French Revolution ideals.
- **Leader**: **Toussaint Louverture** led the revolt.
- **Effects**: Defeated the French; created **first Black government** in Western Hemisphere; **only truly successful large-scale slave rebellion** in world history.

#### Latin American Revolutions
- **Causes**: Enlightenment ideas + resentment of imperial control.
- **Trigger**: Napoleon invaded Spain (1808) → instability in colonies.
- **Document**: ***Letter from Jamaica*** by **Simon Bolivar** ⭐ — called for unity against Spanish rule; challenged mercantilism.
- **Effects**: Long wars; many new republican governments.

#### Philippine Propaganda Movement
- Nationalist movement (not a revolution).
- Filipinos studied Enlightenment ideas in Europe → published pamphlets demanding political participation → eventually led to **Philippine Revolution** (late 19th c.).

---

### 🏴 Nationalist Unification Movements

- **Germany**: 39 fragmented states → Prussia led wars to unify; shared language + culture.
- **Italy**: Fragmented states → military leaders united through wars; shared Italian identity.

### 🏭 The Industrial Revolution

#### 7 Factors That Enabled British Industrialization ⭐

1. **Proximity to waterways**: Island + abundant rivers/canals → efficient transport.
2. **Geographical distribution of coal, iron, timber**: All raw materials available.
3. **Access to foreign resources**: Cotton from India for textile industry (first to industrialize).
4. **Improved agricultural productivity**: More food → growing population.
5. **Urbanization**: People moved to cities seeking factory jobs → labor supply.
6. **Legal protections of private property**: Safe to take entrepreneurial risks.
7. **Accumulation of capital**: Wealth from Atlantic Slave Trade + colonial ventures → startup money.

#### How Production Changed
- **Factories**: Mass production → costs dropped.
- Water-powered → **steam engine** (James Watt) → factories could be anywhere.
- Labor became **increasingly specialized** (one repetitive task, not whole product).

#### How Industrialization Spread

| Country | Key Features |
|---|---|
| **France** | Slow — lacked coal/iron; government sponsored railroads and canals |
| **USA** | Accelerated by Civil War; high resources + political stability → highest worker standard of living |
| **Russia** | State-driven; Trans-Siberian Railroad; brutal worker conditions; frequent uprisings |
| **Japan** | **Meiji Restoration**: State-sponsored defensive industrialization → most successful globally |

> [!warning] Key Shift
> India and Egypt — long known for textile production — saw their **global share decline** as Britain's rose.

---

### ⚙️ Industrial Technology

#### First Industrial Revolution
- **Power**: Coal + steam (James Watt's steam engine) → locomotives + steamships.
- **Building material**: Iron.

#### Second Industrial Revolution
- **Power**: Oil (internal combustion engine) + electricity (Edison's lightbulb → electric streetcars, subways).
- **Communication**: **Telegraph** (Morse Code).
- **Building material**: **Steel** (Bessemer process).
- **Maritime**: Iron/steel ships → **Suez Canal**.

---

### ⚡ Reactions to Industrialization

#### Working-Class Conditions
- Lived in **tenements**; diseases spread; long, dangerous, low-paid work.
- Life expectancy dropped from **40 to 30**; crime rates rose.

#### Government Responses (England)
- Expanded suffrage; restricted child labor; opened public schools; built infrastructure; limited work hours.

#### Working-Class Self-Help
- **Social societies**: Insurance + community.
- **Labor unions**: Collective bargaining for better wages, conditions, hours.

#### Marxism
- **Karl Marx**: Proletariat (working class) will rise up, overthrow the bourgeoisie (capitalists), take over **means of production** → **classless society**.
- **Socialism** = umbrella term. **Communism** = form of socialism requiring **violent revolution**.

#### Reactionary Industrialization
- **Qing China — Self-Strengthening Movement**: Borrowed from West while preserving Chinese culture. Conservatives resisted → halfhearted → China lost **Sino-Japanese War**.
- **Ottoman Empire — Tanzimat Reforms**: Built factories + railroads; western-style laws + constitution. Conservatives resisted → Sultan resumed **absolute monarchy**.
