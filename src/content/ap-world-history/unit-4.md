# Unit 4: Transoceanic Interconnections (1450–1750 CE)

## 🧠 Core Idea

European exploration from 1450–1750 fundamentally transformed the world by connecting the Eastern and Western Hemispheres for the first time. The Columbian Exchange transferred diseases, crops, and animals. New labor systems — including chattel slavery — were imposed to fuel colonial economies. Social hierarchies were reshaped, new religions spread and syncretized, and states competed for maritime dominance.

---

## ⚡ Key Takeaways

- European exploration was driven by **technology** (compass, astrolabe, caravel), **economics** (mercantilism, joint-stock companies), and **politics** (state centralization).
- The **Columbian Exchange** was environmental, not merely economic: diseases, plants, and animals all crossed the Atlantic.
- **90%** of indigenous Americans died from Old World diseases → the "Great Dying."
- The Atlantic System created a new global economy built on **coerced labor**: indigenous forced labor, chattel slavery, indentured servitude, encomienda, hacienda.
- Chattel slavery was **race-based and hereditary** — a new and more brutal form than previous slavery.
- The **Casta System** in Spanish America ranked people by racial purity.
- Cultural synthesis created syncretic religions (e.g., **Vodun**) and new languages (Creole).
- Japan, France (Fronde), and enslaved Africans (maroon societies) all **resisted** imperial expansion.

## 📑 Section Breakdown

### ⛵ Causes of European Exploration

#### Maritime Technology
- **Adopted**: Magnetic compass (China); Astrolabe (Greece/Arab world); Lateen sail (Arab world).
- **European innovation**: **Portuguese Caravel** — small, fast, navigated rivers and shallow areas.

#### Political & Economic Motives
- Monarchs centralizing power → wealth to fund exploration.
- **Mercantilism**: Finite wealth in the world → hoard gold/silver; maintain favorable trade balance (export more than import).
- **Colonies**: Created captive markets for imperial exports.
- **Joint-Stock Companies**: Limited liability; investors only risk their individual contribution.
  - Key example: **Dutch East India Company (VOC)**, chartered 1602 — dominated Indian Ocean trade.

---

### 🌐 Establishing Maritime Empires

| Power | Strategy | Key Outcomes |
|---|---|---|
| **Portugal** | Trading post empire along African coast + Indian Ocean | "Factories" (trading posts); caravels + gunpowder; controlled trade not land |
| **Spain** | Columbus → colonization of Americas + Philippines | Demanded tribute, used coerced labor |
| **France** | North Atlantic sea route search | Found Canada → **fur trade**; focused on trade not colonization |
| **England** | Explored Americas as Spain weakened | Roanoke (disaster); **Jamestown 1607** (successful) |
| **Dutch** | Created VOC after independence from Spain | Challenged Spain/Portugal; settled New Amsterdam; achieved **Indian Ocean spice monopoly** |

> [!note] Prince Henry the Navigator
> Portuguese prince who assembled sailors, mapmakers, and shipbuilders. Directed African coast exploration → Indian Ocean. Foundational to Portuguese maritime empire.

---

### 🌱 The Columbian Exchange

**Definition**: Transfer of diseases, foods, plants, and animals between hemispheres — **environmental**, not just trade.

#### Diseases: Afro-Eurasia → Americas
- Smallpox, measles, malaria. Native Americans had **zero immunity** → **90% death rate** → "The Great Dying."

#### Foods: Afro-Eurasia → Americas
- From Europe: wheat, olives, grapes. From Africa/Asia: rice, bananas, sugar. Effect: diversified diets → slowly increased lifespans.

#### Foods: Americas → Afro-Eurasia ⭐
- **Maize (corn) and potatoes** → healthier populations → **population explosion after 1700**.

#### Foods brought by enslaved Africans
- **Okra and rice** — parents braided food into children's hair in case of kidnapping.

#### Animals: Afro-Eurasia → Americas
- Pigs, sheep, cattle, **horses**. Horses transformed indigenous hunting and warfare.

#### Cash Cropping
- Demand for New World crops → **cash cropping** (e.g., sugar in Caribbean).
- Grown on plantations by **enslaved people**; exported to Middle East and Europe.

### 👷 Labor Systems in the Americas

| System | Who | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Mit'a** (adapted) | Spanish (from Inca) | Native Americans forced into Spanish **silver mines** part of year. Inca = public good; Spanish = private profit. |
| **Encomienda** | Spanish | Indigenous provided labor in exchange for food/protection. Similar to feudalism. |
| **Hacienda** | Spanish | Large plantations; forced Native American labor. |
| **Chattel Slavery** | British/Dutch/Spanish | Owned as **property**; race-based and hereditary. Massive scale. |
| **Indentured Servitude** | British | 7-year labor contract; then theoretically free. North America. |

**Key changes from Chattel Slavery**:
- Pre-1500 slavery was **not race-based**.
- Gender imbalance in West Africa → rise of **polygyny**.
- **Blackness became identified with enslavement** — justification for brutality.
- **Cultural synthesis**: Creole languages (European + African + indigenous) in Caribbean and Brazil.

---

### 🛡️ Resistance to Imperial Expansion

- **Japan (Tokugawa)**: Mass Christian conversions seen as threat; expelled missionaries; **near-complete isolation** — but continued trading with **Dutch only**.
- **Fronde Rebellion (France, 1648)**: Louis XIV raised taxes; nobles led **six-year peasant rebellion** → eventually crushed.
- **Maroon Societies**: Escaped enslaved Africans formed independent villages. In Jamaica, **Queen Nanny** led successful rebellion → British and Maroons signed **treaty** recognizing freedom.

---

### 🌍 African States and the Atlantic Economy

- **Asante Empire**: Traded ivory, gold, enslaved people to Europeans → expanded military and political power.
- **Kingdom of Kongo**: Converted to **Christianity** to facilitate Portuguese trade. Trade initially expanded Kongolese power and wealth.

---

### 🌐 The Atlantic System

- Movement of goods, wealth, and laborers between hemispheres → vast increases in wealth.
- Key goods: **sugarcane** (plantations) and **silver** (mined by coerced labor in Bolivia/Peru).
- Silver made Spain rich + satisfied **China's immense demand for silver**.

---

### 🏛️ Changing Social Hierarchies and Beliefs

#### The Casta System
- Rank based on **racial ancestry** — purity of Spanish blood.
- Small group of Spanish elites at top. Some flexibility based on wealth.

#### Christianity and Syncretism
- **Jesuit missionaries** spread Christianity to American colonies.
- Some created **syncretic faiths** blending Christianity + indigenous practices.
- Africans created **Vodun**: African animism + Christianity.
- Catholic Church brought the **Inquisition** to root out "heresy."

#### Treatment of Jews
- Spain/Portugal: Expelled all Jews after Reconquista.
- **Ottoman Empire**: Opened doors to Jewish refugees — contrast in tolerance.
