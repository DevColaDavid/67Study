# Unit 2: Networks of Exchange (1200–1450 CE)

## 🧠 Core Idea

Between 1200–1450, trade networks across Afro-Eurasia **expanded dramatically** thanks to new technology, commercial innovations, and the Mongol Empire's facilitation of safe travel. These networks spread goods, religions, diseases, and ideas across vast distances — with transformative consequences for cities, states, and populations.

---

## ⚡ Key Takeaways

- Trade routes = "networks of exchange." They expanded c. 1200–1450, connecting more of Afro-Eurasia.
- **3 Major Networks of Exchange**:
  - The **Silk Road** moved luxury goods (silk, porcelain); caravanserai and paper money made it possible.
  - The **Indian Ocean** was the high-volume route; monsoon winds, the compass, and astrolabe enabled it.
  - The **Trans-Saharan** network made Mali fabulously wealthy; improved camel saddles were the key tech.
- Cultural consequences: Islam spread to West Africa and SE Asia; Buddhism traveled China-ward; Greek texts came back to Europe.
- The **Mongols** were the ultimate facilitators — Pax Mongolica made Silk Road trade safe and profitable.
- The Bubonic Plague was the darkest consequence of increased connectivity.
- Ibn Battuta, Marco Polo, and Margery Kempe documented this era firsthand.

---

## 📑 Section Breakdown

### 🛤️ The Silk Road

- **Primary goods**: Luxury items — Chinese silk, porcelain — because the dangerous journey required high-profit goods.
- **Impact**: Increased demand → increased production. Chinese farmers shifted from food crops to luxury goods.
- **Caravanserai**: Inns/guesthouses a day's travel apart along the Silk Road. Kept merchants safe; enabled cross-cultural exchange.
- **Commercial innovations**:
  - **Paper money** (China) made currency portable.
  - **Flying Cash/Money System** (China): Deposit in one city, withdraw in another — proto-banking.
  - **Bills of exchange** (Europe): Like modern checks; managed credit.
- **Kashgar**: Where two major Silk Road routes met → grew rich as a merchant hub.

---

### 🌊 The Indian Ocean Network

- **Monsoon winds**: Blew predictably each season — merchants planned voyages around this.
- **Goods**: Bulk items (textiles, spices) AND luxury goods.
- **Tech innovations**: Improved **magnetic compass** → direction; improved **astrolabe** → latitude; **Chinese junks** → big cargo holds.
- **Effects of expansion**:
  - **Swahili City-States**: Grew as important ports linked to Dar al-Islam.
  - **Diasporic communities**: Arab and Persian merchants settled in East Africa → spread **Islam** + created **Swahili language** (Bantu + Arabic).
- **Zheng He**: Ming Dynasty sailor expanding China's **tributary system**. Spread navigation tools and shipbuilding methods.

---

### 🐪 Trans-Saharan Trade Networks

- **Key innovation**: Improved **camel saddles** → larger cargo loads across desert.
- **Mali**: Grew rich by connecting to Dar al-Islam (gold trade) and taxing trade routes.
  - **Mansa Musa** (1324 pilgrimage to Mecca): Displayed incredible wealth; expanded trade networks.

---

### 🌐 Consequences of Connectivity

- **Religion spread**: Islam and Hinduism → Southeast Asia; Buddhism → China via Silk Road.
- **Literary transfers**: Greek/Roman works translated to Arabic at the **House of Wisdom** → back to Europe → **Renaissance**.
- **Gunpowder**: Most significant tech transfer — China → west via Muslims and Mongols.
- **City growth**: Hangzhou (China) grew at Grand Canal's end.
- **City decline**: Baghdad destroyed by Mongols in **1258**.
- **Key travelers**: **Ibn Battuta** ⭐ (Muslim, 30 years, documented Dar al-Islam); **Marco Polo** (European in China); **Margery Kempe** (Christian pilgrim).
- **Environmental**:
  - **Bubonic Plague**: Traveled from China along Silk Road → Middle East and Europe. **Darkest consequence of connectivity.**
  - **Champa rice**: Came to China via tribute system from Champa Kingdom → population growth.

---

### 🏹 The Mongol Empire

- **Why in Unit 2**: Their empire **facilitated the trade connections** of this era.
- **Political impact**: Largest land-based empire ever. Toppled Song Dynasty and Abbasid Empire. Ruled through **Khanates** led by **Khans**.
- **Pax Mongolica**: Mongols paid high prices for foreign goods + kept Silk Road **safe**.
- **Cultural transfers**: Created conditions for transfer of Greek/Islamic medical knowledge to Europe. Adopted **Uyghur script** to create their written language.
