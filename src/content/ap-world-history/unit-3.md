# Unit 3: Land-Based Empires (1450–1750 CE)

## 🧠 Core Idea

From 1450–1750, three great "Gunpowder Empires" — the **Ottomans**, **Safavids**, and **Mughals** — expanded using military technology and then consolidated power through bureaucracy, religious legitimacy, monumental architecture, and tax systems. Their rivalries were deeply intertwined with the Sunni–Shi'a split. In Europe, monarchs used Divine Right and architecture to consolidate absolute power.

---

## ⚡ Key Takeaways

- **Gunpowder = expansion engine** for all three major empires of this era.
- The Ottoman **Devshirme System** created both the Janissaries (elite military) and a loyal bureaucracy from enslaved Christian boys.
- The **Ottoman–Safavid rivalry** was political AND religious: Sunni vs. Shi'a.
- The **Mughal–Safavid conflict** was about land (Afghanistan) but overlaid with Sunni vs. Shi'a tension.
- Legitimizing power = making people accept your authority. Consolidating power = taking power FROM others.
- Rulers used religion, art, architecture, and tax systems to stay in power.
- The **Protestant Reformation** was caused by Catholic corruption; the printing press spread Luther's ideas rapidly.
- **Sikhism** emerged as a syncretic blend of Hinduism and Islam in South Asia.

## 📑 Section Breakdown

### 🔫 Empires Expand (Gunpowder Empires)

#### The Ottoman Empire
- Used **gunpowder and cannons** to control Southwestern Europe and Anatolia.
- **1453**: Conquered Constantinople → renamed **Istanbul**. ⭐ Key date.
- Enslaved Christians from the Balkans → converted to Islam → became the **Janissaries**.
- The enslavement system = **Devshirme System**.

#### The Safavid Empire
- Raided and conquered neighboring territories using **gunpowder weapons**.
- **Shah Abbas** built up military with gunpowder + created enslaved army of Christians from the **Caucasus**.

#### The Mughal Empire
- **Babur** (first leader) defeated the **Delhi Sultanate** → began Mughal expansion.
- **Emperor Akbar** legitimized rule via **religious tolerance** toward the Hindu majority.

---

### ⚔️ Rivalries Between Empires

#### Ottoman–Safavid Rivalry
- Ottomans = **Sunni**; Safavids = **Shi'a**.
- Divide originated as a political dispute over who should rule after Muhammad's death.
- Both believed they were the true representatives of Islam.

#### Safavid–Mughal Conflict
- Wars over lands in **modern Afghanistan**.
- Religious overlay: Safavids (**Shi'a**) vs. Mughals (**Sunni**).

---

### 🏛️ The Administration of Empires

> [!note] Critical Definitions
> - **Legitimizing power**: Methods rulers use to *establish* authority.
> - **Consolidating power**: Methods rulers use to *transfer* power from other groups to themselves.

#### Ottoman Devshirme System
- Enslaved + converted Christian boys from the Balkans.
- Educated and trained in Istanbul.
- Brightest → elite **bureaucratic positions**.
- Also produced the **Janissaries** (elite military).
- Result: loyal bureaucrats + soldiers with no family loyalties to threaten the sultan.

#### Religious Legitimacy
- **Divine Right of Kings** (Europe): Kings = God's representatives. Questioning king = questioning God.
- **Qing Dynasty** (China): Manchu rulers depicted in portraits holding **Confucian texts** to appeal to Han majority.

---

### ✝️ Belief Systems in Empires

#### The Protestant Reformation
- **Catholic corruption**: **Simony** (buying Church offices) + **indulgences** (paying for sin forgiveness).
- **Martin Luther**: Found these practices weren't in the Bible → published **95 Theses**.
- **Printing press** rapidly spread his ideas → **Protestant Reformation** → new Christian churches.
- **Catholic Reformation**: **Council of Trent** cleaned up corruption, kept core beliefs.
- Both contributed to growth of Christianity. (**Change AND continuity**.)

#### Deepening Sunni–Shi'a Split
- **Shah Ismail** declared Safavid Empire a **Shi'a state**.
- Sunni Muslims publicly executed.
- Safavid military cursed first three caliphs as illegitimate.

#### Sikhism — A New Syncretic Religion
- From **Islam**: Belief in one God.
- From **Hinduism**: Cycle of reincarnation.
- Unique features: Rejected **gender hierarchies** and the **caste system** → radical equality.
