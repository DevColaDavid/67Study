# Unit 8: Cold War and Decolonization (1900–Present)

## 🧠 Core Idea

After WWII, the US and Soviet Union emerged as rival superpowers locked in an ideological struggle between **democratic capitalism** and **authoritarian communism** — the Cold War. Simultaneously, a massive wave of **decolonization** swept Asia and Africa. The paths to independence varied dramatically — from negotiated peace (India) to bloody armed struggle (Algeria). Resistance to existing power structures took many forms.

---

## ⚡ Key Takeaways

- Cold War = ideological struggle (capitalism vs. communism) + mutual mistrust, NOT direct combat.
- The US became a superpower through economic dominance, the Marshall Plan, and the atomic bomb.
- The USSR became a superpower through centralized economic recovery and its own atomic bomb (1949).
- Cold War produced: **NATO**, **Warsaw Pact**, and dozens of deadly **proxy wars** (Korea, Vietnam, Nicaragua, Angola).
- **Mutually Assured Destruction (MAD)** kept the US and USSR from direct war.
- **Decolonization** driven by war-weakened Europe, unfulfilled promises to colonial troops, Enlightenment ideas.
- **Gandhi, MLK Jr., and Nelson Mandela** are specifically named in the AP Exam Description. ⭐
- Inherited colonial borders created new states with built-in ethnic/religious conflict (India–Pakistan, Israel–Palestine).

## 📑 Section Breakdown

### 🌍 Rise of Superpowers

#### Why the US Became a Superpower
- **Economic**: WWII skyrocketed industrial production; US in far better shape than devastated Europe.
- **Marshall Plan**: Gave money to western Europe to rebuild → Europe became dependent on US.
- **Military**: Had the **atomic bomb** — no one else did (until 1949).

#### Why the USSR Became a Superpower
- **Economy**: Centralized command economy directed resources toward recovery.
- **Military**: In **August 1949** tested own atomic bomb → achieved military parity.

#### Why Decolonization Happened After WWII
- Colonial troops fought in both WWIs without a firm independence promise.
- After WWII: **Europe too economically and militarily weakened** to suppress independence movements.

---

### ❄️ The Cold War — Causes and Effects

#### Two Major Causes
1. **Conflicting ideologies**: US (democratic capitalism) vs. USSR (authoritarian communism) — both universalizing.
2. **Mutual mistrust**: Stalin broke promises about free elections in Eastern Europe → those countries forced into communism → became **Soviet satellite states**.

#### Two Major Effects
1. **Military alliances**: **NATO** (1949: US + allies) vs. **Warsaw Pact** (USSR + satellite states). Both = attack one, attack all. Nuclear arsenals → **MAD**.
2. **Proxy wars**: Ideological struggle carried out in smaller conflicts across Latin America, Africa, Asia.

#### Non-Aligned Movement
- Led by **Indonesian president Achmad Sukarno** (1955).
- Nations refusing to choose between US and USSR → became known as **"Third World" countries**.

### 🌏 Proxy Wars — Key Examples

| War | US Side | USSR Side |
|---|---|---|
| **Korea** (1950–53) | Anti-communist South | Communist North; war ended in division at **38th parallel** |
| **Nicaragua / Contra War** (1979–90) | Anti-communist Contras | Sandinistas (socialist); backed by Cuba + USSR |
| **Angola Civil War** (1975–2002) | Anti-communist groups | Communist groups |

### 🔴 The Spread of Communism

#### China Becomes Communist
- Civil war: Nationalist Party (**Chiang Kai-Shek**) vs. Communist Party (**Mao Zedong**).
- Paused during WWII → resumed → Communists (supported by USSR) won.
- **Effects**: Collectivization; **Great Leap Forward** → failed → famine killing **20–50 million people**.

#### Vietnam
- After WWII: French colony → declared independence → two rival governments (communist north, anti-communist south) → proxy war.

#### Cuba
- **1956**: **Fidel Castro** led communist revolution. Ended Cuba's economic reliance on US; allied with USSR; redistributed land + wages; **nationalized** US corporate-owned land.
- CIA tried to overthrow Castro → only further radicalized him.

#### Egypt (Socialism)
- **Gamal Abdel Nasser**: Redistributed land + resources; **nationalized the Suez Canal**. Backed by USSR → Egypt won.

---

### 🧊 The End of the Cold War

#### Four Causes of Soviet Collapse ⭐
1. **Reagan's "Star Wars"** (SDI): Soviets tried to match US weapons spending → couldn't afford it → economic decline.
2. **Soviet–Afghan War (1979–88)**: Soviets supported Afghan communist government; **Mujahideen** (backed by US, Saudi Arabia, Pakistan) fought back → nine-year war → depressed Soviet economy.
3. **Gorbachev's reforms**: **Perestroika** (economic restructuring, less central planning) + **Glasnost** (free speech → people expressed anger at government).
4. **USSR stopped propping up communist governments** → democratic movements spread across Soviet bloc.

#### How the USSR Collapsed
- Soviet bloc states → democratic reform movements → Soviet republics declared independence.
- **1989**: **Berlin Wall torn down**; Germany reunified.
- **1991**: Soviet legislature **voted to dissolve the Soviet Union** — Cold War ended.

### 🌱 Decolonization — Paths to Independence

#### India — Negotiated Independence (1947)
- **Indian National Congress** (INC) formed 1885; repeatedly denied independence.
- Post-WWII: Britain broke and pro-independence Parliament → INC negotiated independence.
- **Partition of India**: Muslim minority → **Muslim League** → demanded separate state → **Pakistan** created.
- **Violence**: Hindus fled south; Muslims fled north. **Hundreds of thousands — possibly over a million** — died.

#### Algeria — Armed Struggle (Independence 1962)
- France refused to negotiate with Algeria (many French people lived there).
- **1954**: **National Liberation Front (FLN)** formed → violence against French troops and civilians.
- France responded brutally → one of the **bloodiest independence movements** in history.
- **1962**: President **Charles de Gaulle** finally negotiated → Algeria independent.

#### Israel — Inherited Conflict
- Palestine was mostly Muslim. **Zionist movement** (late 19th c.) advocated Jewish state in Palestine.
- After WWI: Palestine became **British mandate**. **Balfour Declaration** supported Jewish state.
- Post-WWII: Jewish migration spiked (fleeing Holocaust).
- **1948**: UN partition plan accepted by Jews → **Israel declared independent**. Palestinians refused; Arab neighbors attacked → ongoing conflict that continues today.

---

### 🌍 Post-Decolonization: Economies and Migration

#### Government Economic Control — India Example
- **Indira Gandhi** (first and only female PM of India): Socialist five-year economic plans + embraced the **Green Revolution** (genetically engineered seeds + fertilizers → dramatically increased agricultural yields).

#### Migration to Metropoles
- Colonial subjects migrated to the **metropole** (home territory of former imperial power) seeking work.
- South Asians → Great Britain; Algerians → France; Filipinos → United States.
- **Effects**: Maintained cultural/economic ties with former colony; transformed formerly homogenous societies into **multi-ethnic societies**.

### ✊ Resistance Movements

> [!warning] AP Exam Required ⭐
> **Mohandas Gandhi**, **Martin Luther King Jr.**, and **Nelson Mandela** are specifically named in the AP CED as people who promoted nonviolence. You MUST know all three.

#### Mohandas Gandhi — India ⭐
- Prominent INC leader; devoted to **civil disobedience** (nonviolent law-breaking).
- **Homespun Movement**: Protested British domination of India's cotton industry.
- **Salt March**: Against British Salt Act — Gandhi + 50,000–60,000 marched to coast; dug up salt deposits; many beaten and arrested including Gandhi.
- His efforts began to **break Britain's colonial hold on India**.

#### Martin Luther King Jr. — United States ⭐
- Led **Black Americans in the Civil Rights Movement**; inspired by Gandhi.
- **Montgomery Bus Boycott**: Boycotted segregated public transportation in Alabama → economic distress → spread.
- **Outcomes**: Supreme Court outlawed school racial discrimination (1950s); Congress passed **Civil Rights Act (1960s)**.

#### Nelson Mandela — South Africa ⭐
- **Apartheid**: Segregation laws denying basic rights to Black South Africans.
- Mandela led **African National Congress (ANC)**; led strikes and boycotts.
- **Sharpeville Massacre**: Police killed 69 nonviolent protesters → Mandela abandoned nonviolence.
- Jailed **over 20 years**.
- **1994**: Won election → apartheid ended.

#### Pinochet — Chile (Violence Intensifying Conflict)
- **Augusto Pinochet** (with **US support**) launched military coup against Marxist president **Salvador Allende**.
- Became dictator; violently suppressed opposition with military raids, executions, and torture.

#### Terrorism as Resistance
- **Shining Path** (Peru); **IRA** (Ireland); **Al Qaeda** (global, led by **Osama bin Laden**) → **September 11, 2001 attacks** (2,000+ Americans killed).
